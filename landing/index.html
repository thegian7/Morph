How it works

Colors shift gradually—your brain absorbs the change without ever having to check the time.

Situation Color You feel
Nothing for 60+ min Deep focus zone
Meeting in ~30 min Subconscious nudge
Meeting in ~15 min Body starts preparing
Meeting in ~5 min Time to wrap up
Meeting in ~2 min Transition imminent
In a meeting (late) Approaching the end
Overtime Time's up—gently

The science

This isn't a guess. There's real research behind ambient color as a time cue.

Peripheral color works

Timeqube's EU-funded R&D found that ambient colors in peripheral vision produce awareness without stress. Your brain registers the shift without conscious effort.

Color before text

The human visual system processes color before shape or text. A green-to-amber shift reaches your brain faster and more gently than any notification.

Clinically recommended

Visual timers are a clinically recommended intervention for ADHD time blindness. The same dopamine systems respond well to ambient environmental cues.

Morph is in early beta

It works. We use it daily. But there are a few things to know before you install:

Google Calendar: "Unverified app" warning

Google's verification process takes weeks. Morph only reads your event times and titles to determine border colors. No data leaves your machine. Click Advanced → Go to Morph (unsafe) to proceed.

macOS: Gatekeeper warning

Not yet signed with an Apple Developer certificate. Right-click the app → Open → confirm. You only need to do this once.

Windows: SmartScreen warning

Click More info → Run anyway.

We're being transparent about these because we'd rather earn your trust than hide the rough edges.
